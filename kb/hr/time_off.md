# Time Off Policies

This document outlines all company time off policies including holidays, vacation, sick leave, and other leave types.

## Vacation

### Accrual Schedule

Vacation time accrues based on length of service:

| Years of Service | Annual Accrual | Accrual Rate (per pay period) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 4.62 hours |
| 3-5 years | 20 days (160 hours) | 6.15 hours |
| 6+ years | 25 days (200 hours) | 7.69 hours |

### Accrual Rules

- Vacation begins accruing on your first day of employment
- Accruals are calculated on a bi-weekly basis (26 pay periods per year)
- Part-time employees accrue vacation on a prorated basis
- Vacation accrual is paused during unpaid leave

### Using Vacation Time

- **Minimum increment:** Vacation may be taken in minimum increments of 4 hours
- **Advance notice:** Provide at least 2 weeks notice for requests of 3+ consecutive days
- **Manager approval:** All vacation requests require manager approval via the HRIS system
- **Blackout periods:** Project-critical periods may restrict vacation availability; consult your manager

### Carryover and Maximum Balance

- **Maximum carryover:** Up to 5 unused vacation days may be carried over to the following year
- **Maximum balance cap:** Vacation stops accruing when balance reaches 1.5x annual accrual
- **Use-it-or-lose-it:** Days exceeding the carryover limit expire on December 31

### Vacation Payout

- Upon termination, accrued but unused vacation will be paid out at your current rate
- Employees must provide at least 2 weeks notice to receive vacation payout

## Sick Days

### Annual Allocation

All full-time employees receive **8 sick days (64 hours)** per calendar year, allocated on January 1.

- New hires receive a prorated allocation based on start date
- Part-time employees receive prorated sick time based on scheduled hours
- Sick days do not accrue; the full allocation is available immediately

### Acceptable Use

Sick time may be used for:

- Personal illness or injury
- Medical, dental, or vision appointments
- Care for an immediate family member who is ill
- Mental health days
- Preventive care and wellness visits

### Reporting Absences

- Notify your manager as early as possible, preferably before your scheduled start time
- For absences of 3+ consecutive days, a doctor's note may be required
- Log all sick time in the HRIS system within 48 hours

### Carryover

- Unused sick days do **not** carry over to the following year
- Sick days are not paid out upon termination

## Bereavement

The company provides paid bereavement leave to support employees during difficult times.

### Leave Allowance

| Relationship | Paid Days |
|--------------|-----------|
| Spouse/domestic partner | 5 days |
| Child (including stepchild) | 5 days |
| Parent (including stepparent) | 5 days |
| Sibling | 3 days |
| Grandparent | 3 days |
| Grandchild | 3 days |
| In-laws (parent, sibling) | 3 days |
| Other close relation | 1 day |

### Guidelines

- Bereavement leave does not need to be taken consecutively
- Leave should be taken within 30 days of the loss
- Additional unpaid time or use of vacation may be approved by your manager
- Notify HR and your manager as soon as practicable
- The company may request documentation (e.g., obituary, funeral program) for leaves exceeding 3 days

## Day of Service

The company encourages community involvement by providing **one paid Day of Service** per calendar year.

### Eligibility

- All full-time and part-time employees are eligible after 90 days of employment

### Guidelines

- Volunteer activities must be with a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization
- Service may be completed individually or as part of a company-organized event
- Submit your planned activity for manager approval at least 1 week in advance
- After completing your service, submit a brief summary to HR

### Examples of Qualifying Activities

- Food bank or shelter volunteering
- Habitat for Humanity builds
- Environmental cleanup events
- Mentorship programs
- Board service for nonprofits (meeting attendance)

## Jury Duty

The company supports employees fulfilling their civic responsibilities.

### Policy

- Employees receive **full pay** for up to **10 business days** of jury duty per year
- For jury service exceeding 10 days, employees will receive the difference between jury duty pay and regular salary
- Employees must provide a copy of the jury summons to HR

### Expectations

- Notify your manager immediately upon receiving a jury summons
- On days when jury service ends early or you are not required to report, return to work if practical
- Provide documentation of service dates upon completion

## Family Leave

The company provides family leave in accordance with the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) and offers enhanced benefits beyond federal requirements.

### Eligibility

To be eligible for family leave, employees must:

- Have worked for the company for at least 12 months
- Have worked at least 1,250 hours in the preceding 12 months

### Parental Leave

**Birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child:**

| Leave Type | Duration | Pay |
|------------|----------|-----|
| Primary caregiver | 8 weeks | 100% paid |
| Secondary caregiver | 4 weeks | 100% paid |
| Additional FMLA leave | Up to 4 weeks | Unpaid |

- Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of birth, adoption, or placement
- Leave may be taken consecutively or intermittently with manager approval
- Both parents are eligible if both work for the company

### Medical Leave (Self)

- Up to **12 weeks** of job-protected leave for a serious health condition
- **First 2 weeks:** 100% paid (short-term disability bridge)
- **Weeks 3-12:** Eligible for short-term disability benefits (60% of salary)

### Family Medical Leave (Caring for Others)

- Up to **12 weeks** of job-protected leave to care for a spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition
- **First 2 weeks:** 100% paid
- **Weeks 3-12:** Unpaid, but employees may use accrued vacation or sick time

### How to Request Leave

1. Notify HR at least 30 days in advance when leave is foreseeable
2. Complete the Family Leave Request Form in the HRIS system
3. Provide medical certification if applicable
4. Work with your manager to create a coverage plan

### Benefits During Leave

- Health insurance coverage continues during approved family leave
- Employee remains responsible for their portion of premium contributions
- Vacation and sick time do not accrue during unpaid leave

### Return to Work

- Employees are guaranteed return to the same or equivalent position
- A fitness-for-duty certification may be required for medical leave
