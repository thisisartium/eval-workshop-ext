# Code of Conduct

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards and professional expectations for all employees. Every team member is responsible for understanding and following these guidelines.

---

## Our Values

Our Code of Conduct is built on these core values:

- **Integrity** - We act honestly and ethically in all business dealings
- **Respect** - We treat everyone with dignity and professionalism
- **Excellence** - We deliver high-quality work and continuously improve
- **Collaboration** - We work together openly and supportively
- **Accountability** - We take responsibility for our actions and decisions

---

## Professional Conduct

### Workplace Behavior

All employees are expected to:

- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and courtesy
- Communicate professionally in all interactions (email, Slack, meetings)
- Be punctual and prepared for meetings and commitments
- Follow through on commitments and meet deadlines
- Provide constructive feedback respectfully
- Accept feedback gracefully and use it for growth

### Dress Code

- **In-office:** Business casual
- **Client meetings:** Match or exceed client dress code
- **Remote work:** Professional appearance for video calls
- Use good judgment for your audience and context

### Communication Standards

- Respond to internal messages within **one business day**
- Respond to client communications within **4 hours** during business hours
- Use appropriate channels (urgent matters via Slack/phone, not email)
- Keep communications professional, concise, and clear
- Avoid sarcasm and humor that may not translate well in writing

## Anti-Harassment and Non-Discrimination

### Policy Statement

The company is committed to providing a work environment free from discrimination and harassment. We prohibit discrimination and harassment based on:

- Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin
- Religion or religious practices
- Sex, gender, gender identity, or gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Genetic information
- Veteran or military status
- Marital or family status
- Pregnancy or related conditions
- Any other protected characteristic under applicable law

### Prohibited Conduct

**Harassment** includes any unwelcome conduct that:
- Creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment
- Unreasonably interferes with work performance
- Affects employment decisions

**Examples of prohibited behavior:**
- Offensive jokes, slurs, or name-calling
- Physical assaults, threats, or intimidation
- Unwanted sexual advances or requests for sexual favors
- Displaying offensive images or objects
- Mocking or ridiculing someone's protected characteristics
- Cyberbullying or online harassment

### Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome:
- Sexual advances or propositions
- Requests for sexual favors
- Verbal conduct (comments about appearance, sexual jokes)
- Non-verbal conduct (leering, gestures, displaying images)
- Physical conduct (touching, blocking movement)

Sexual harassment is prohibited regardless of the gender of the individuals involved.

### Reporting Harassment

If you experience or witness harassment:

1. **Document** the incident(s) with dates, times, witnesses, and details
2. **Report** to any of the following:
   - Your manager
   - Any other manager
   - HR at hr@company.com
   - Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX
3. **Cooperate** with any investigation

**Retaliation is prohibited.** The company will not tolerate retaliation against anyone who reports harassment or participates in an investigation.

## Conflicts of Interest

### Definition

A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests interfere—or appear to interfere—with the company's interests.

### Examples

- **Outside employment:** Working for a competitor or client
- **Financial interests:** Owning stock in a competitor, vendor, or client
- **Personal relationships:** Supervising a family member or close friend
- **Gifts and entertainment:** Accepting gifts that could influence decisions
- **Board memberships:** Serving on boards of organizations with business relationships

### Disclosure Requirements

You must disclose potential conflicts to your manager and HR:
- **Before** starting outside employment or board positions
- **Immediately** when a personal relationship could affect work decisions
- **Annually** during the conflict of interest certification process

### Guidelines

- Avoid situations where personal interests could influence business judgment
- Do not use company resources for personal gain
- Do not take business opportunities discovered through your role
- When in doubt, disclose and seek guidance

## Confidentiality and Data Protection

### Confidential Information

Employees must protect confidential information, including:

- Client data, contracts, and business information
- Employee personal information
- Financial data and projections
- Strategic plans and business development
- Proprietary processes and methodologies
- Source code and technical documentation

### Obligations

- **Never share** confidential information with unauthorized parties
- **Secure** confidential documents (lock screens, secure storage)
- **Use only** for legitimate business purposes
- **Return** all confidential materials upon leaving the company
- **Report** suspected breaches immediately to Security

### Client Confidentiality

- Honor all client NDAs and confidentiality agreements
- Do not discuss client work in public spaces
- Do not use client logos or names without permission
- Protect client data according to contractual and regulatory requirements

### Post-Employment

Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends. Do not share or use confidential information from the company or previous employers.

## Use of Company Resources

### Acceptable Use

Company resources (equipment, software, internet, email) are provided for business purposes.

**Limited personal use is permitted** provided it:
- Does not interfere with work
- Does not violate any policies
- Does not consume significant resources
- Is occasional and brief

### Prohibited Uses

- Accessing or distributing illegal content
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying
- Operating a personal business
- Cryptocurrency mining
- Excessive personal use (streaming, gaming, social media)
- Circumventing security controls
- Installing unauthorized software

### Monitoring

The company may monitor use of company resources to:
- Ensure compliance with policies
- Protect company assets and data
- Investigate potential misconduct
- Comply with legal requirements

Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company resources.

## Social Media and Public Communications

### Personal Social Media

When using personal social media:
- **Do not** speak on behalf of the company unless authorized
- **Include disclaimers** if discussing industry topics: "Views are my own"
- **Do not** share confidential or proprietary information
- **Do not** disparage the company, colleagues, clients, or competitors
- **Be mindful** that your online presence can reflect on the company

### Company Social Media

Only authorized employees may post to official company accounts. Follow brand guidelines and approval processes.

### Media and Press Inquiries

Direct all media inquiries to the Communications team. Do not speak to press or analysts without authorization.

## Gifts and Entertainment

### Receiving Gifts

| Gift Value | Policy |
|------------|--------|
| Under $50 | May accept (branded items, meals) |
| $50 - $150 | May accept with manager notification |
| Over $150 | Decline or donate to company raffle |
| Cash or equivalents | Never accept, regardless of amount |

**Always decline gifts that:**
- Could influence a business decision
- Create an appearance of impropriety
- Are offered during active negotiations or procurement
- Violate the giver's own policies

### Giving Gifts

- Follow client gift policies (many prohibit gifts)
- Gifts must be reasonable and appropriate
- Never offer cash or cash equivalents
- Document gifts over $50 in the expense system

### Entertainment

- Business entertainment should be reasonable and infrequent
- Both parties should attend (no tickets to events without you)
- Government officials have strict rules—consult Legal before any entertainment

## Substance Abuse

### Policy

The company is committed to maintaining a safe, drug-free workplace.

- **Prohibited:** Being under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs during work
- **Prohibited:** Using, possessing, or distributing illegal drugs on company premises
- **Permitted:** Moderate alcohol at company events and client entertainment

### Prescription Medications

Employees taking prescription medications that may affect performance or safety should:
- Consult with HR confidentially
- Follow medical guidance
- Notify manager if duties need temporary modification

### Support

Employees struggling with substance abuse are encouraged to seek help through:
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Medical benefits (covered treatment)
- Confidential leave for treatment

## Compliance and Reporting Violations

### Obligation to Report

All employees must report known or suspected violations of:
- This Code of Conduct
- Company policies
- Laws or regulations

### How to Report

- **Manager:** For most concerns
- **HR:** hr@company.com
- **Ethics Hotline:** 1-800-XXX-XXXX (anonymous option available)
- **Legal:** For regulatory or legal concerns

### Investigation Process

1. All reports are taken seriously and investigated promptly
2. Investigations are conducted confidentially to the extent possible
3. Appropriate corrective action is taken when violations are confirmed
4. Reporters are informed of general outcomes when appropriate

### Non-Retaliation

The company strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports a concern in good faith
- Participates in an investigation
- Refuses to participate in wrongdoing

Retaliation is itself a violation of this Code and will result in disciplinary action.

### Consequences of Violations

Violations of this Code may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Required training
- Performance improvement plan
- Demotion or reassignment
- Termination of employment
- Legal action where appropriate
